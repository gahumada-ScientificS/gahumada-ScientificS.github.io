Who This Is For

Scientists at a Crossroads

This is not for everyone — and that is intentional. Career coaching works best when the fit is right. Here is who tends to get the most out of these conversations.

PhD Students & Candidates

Finishing Your PhD and Not Sure What's Next

Academia or industry? Postdoc or direct hire? Which companies are worth targeting? What does your PhD actually translate to in the real world? These are the questions we work through together.

Postdocs & Early-Career Scientists

Ready to Move — But Not Sure Where

You have the technical depth. What you need is clarity on how to position yourself, which sectors actually fit your background, and how to navigate a job market that does not always speak your language.

Scientists Considering Independence

Thinking About Consulting or Starting Something

If you are wondering whether you can turn your scientific expertise into an independent practice — consulting, advising, or building something of your own — this is a conversation worth having before you make any decisions.

How It Works

Simple. Honest. No Commitment to Start.

01

Reach Out

Send a short email or book a free intro call directly via Calendly. Tell me briefly where you are in your career and what you are trying to figure out. No long form, no CV required to start.

02

Free Intro Call — 30 Minutes

We get on a call. You tell me your situation. I ask questions. We figure out together whether these conversations would be useful for you and what format makes sense. There is no pitch and no pressure. If I am not the right person to help you, I will say so.

03

Follow-Up Sessions

If the intro is useful, we continue. This can be a single focused session, a short series over a few weeks, or ongoing check-ins as your situation evolves. We structure it around what actually helps — not a fixed program.

04

Introductions & Next Steps

Where it makes sense, I make introductions from my network. Every follow-up is purposeful — we are always moving you toward something concrete, not just talking in circles.
