Independent Material Qualification & Technical Resolution

Beyond the CoA.
Real Science.
Practical Answers.

A supplier's Certificate of Analysis is a starting point. It confirms what was tested — but it doesn't tell you whether the material is right for your application, your process, or your customers. US buyers and manufacturers increasingly require independent confirmation before they can act on it.

Our role goes well beyond ordering laboratory tests. We start by determining which tests are scientifically relevant for your specific material, its intended use, and your development stage. We then coordinate testing through qualified US analytical laboratories, review the results against supplier documentation, and provide a clear technical interpretation — not just a pass/fail.

And if a material doesn't meet expectations, we don't stop there. We help you understand the root cause and define practical next steps.

When a material doesn't pass — we help you decide what's next
→ Additional analytical testing → Impurity investigation → Supplier communication strategy → Specification revision → Purification strategy → Process troubleshooting → Reformulation support → Alternative vendor identification
  • —Test strategy design based on material, use case & development stage
  • —Coordination with qualified US analytical laboratories
  • —CoA cross-verification & technical interpretation of results
  • —Root cause analysis & resolution roadmap when issues arise
  • —Applicable to chemical, cosmetic, pharmaceutical & specialty materials

Engagement 04

Verifying an Imported Raw Material for the US Market

Supply Chain
The Challenge

A US-based formulator sources a specialty ingredient from an international supplier. The material arrives with a supplier CoA, but their customer requires independent US-laboratory confirmation before it can be used in production.

Our Role

We designed a test strategy relevant to the material's intended use, coordinated testing through a qualified US laboratory, and cross-verified results against the supplier's CoA. When one parameter fell outside specification, we identified the likely root cause and recommended a targeted next step — avoiding a full material rejection.

The Outcome

The client received a clear technical picture of their material — what passed, what didn't, why, and what to do about it. Production continued on a revised specification with full confidence in the material's suitability.
